## Root Causes

### 1. FAK + Latency Race Condition
Scalp orders are FAK (Fill-and-Kill). FAK requires liquidity to exist at the exact microsecond the API processes the order — no match means instant kill. The bot checks the order book, sees ask levels, then spends **650–1150ms** constructing and submitting the order. By then, liquidity has moved. The book check is a stale snapshot, not a reservation.

### 2. Price Cap Too Tight
The bot limits to `max_price = prob_win - min_edge`. In the 1 second of submission latency, the best ask often ticks above this cap — FAK kills the entire order rather than filling at a slightly worse price.

### 3. GTC Fallback Is Priced Too Passively
GTC resting orders were cancelled unfilled across all sessions. They appear placed too far from the market; no taker crosses them before the window ends.

---

## Recommendations

### Option A — Widen the FAK Price Cap (Easiest Win)
Add a small fixed buffer above the calculated `max_price` (e.g., +$0.03–$0.05). The best ask may have moved only 1–3 ticks above the cap during submission latency. A small buffer captures most of those near-misses without materially changing expected value. A fill with slightly less edge beats a 97% miss rate at zero EV.

### Option B — Remove the Price Cap on FAK (More Aggressive)
Place FAK as a true market sweep with no price limit. Slippage is bounded by the book depth already verified at check time. The expected cost of extra slippage is likely far less than the EV cost of a 97% miss rate.

### Option C — Switch to Resting Limit Orders (Structural Fix, Recommended)
Instead of sweeping the ask as a taker, post a **resting GTC limit at the current best ask** (or 1 tick inside it), becoming the liquidity rather than chasing it. This:
- Eliminates the latency race entirely
- Earns maker rebates instead of paying taker fees
- Uses the 5-minute window productively — ample time to get crossed

The current GTC fallback fails because it's priced too passively. Moving it to the best ask level makes fills likely whenever a taker enters the market.

### Option D — Reduce Latency in the FAK Path
Pre-sign the order before the book check, so submission can begin immediately after the check rather than waiting for construction. Trimming 200–400ms from the check-to-submit gap meaningfully reduces the race condition window.

### Option E — Tighten FAK Fire Conditions
Currently the bot fires on any non-zero ask levels. Add stricter filters: only fire when the book has multiple levels **at or below `max_price`** (not just total ask levels), and only when the spread is narrow. Avoid firing into thin or wide books where the race condition is most destructive.

---

## Priority Recommendation

**Combine Option A + Option C:**

1. **Widen the FAK price cap** by a small buffer — should lift fill rate from ~5% to an estimated 20–30% on its own.
2. **Post GTC resting limits at the current best ask** when a FAK misses — gives the fallback a real chance of filling within the window instead of sitting passively and expiring.

The core structural issue is that the current strategy is a liquidity-taker with ~1 second of network latency and no co-location advantage. Becoming a maker (Option C) changes the dynamic fundamentally and is the highest-leverage long-term fix.
